P-CARD PURCHASES:
  1. Before placing your order, please be sure to complete the “Purchasing Card Receipt Form” (Completely)
    1. Remember to have PI or Budget Authority sign where indicated and include the Project that will pay for the order. This ensures that approval was sought beforehand.
    2. NOTE: When completing this form and listing the Items Purchased, please be sure to use layman's terms. We need to code all purchases in the system and the staff in the finance office may not know the research terminology that you are often times used to communicating. Item#s without a description is not helpful.
    3. Provide the vendor with the Ship To and Bill To information. This is VERY important to ensure that you receive your order promptly and that the bill is paid timely.

      4. Ship To:

PURCHASE ORDERS:

A Purchase Order is required if you are purchasing one of the following: book that is $251 or greater, 1 item is over $1,000 and a life expectancy greater than 1 year, or you are adding value to an existing piece of equipment.

  1. Complete a “Order Request Form” (completely)
    1. Remember to have the Principal Investigator or Budget Authority sign where indicated and include the Project that will pay for the order.
  2. Make sure to include an estimated shipping cost for all quotes. If quotes are received without this information, they will be returned to you with a note that all quotes need an estimated shipping cost before a purchase order request can be submitted.
  3. Include necessary quotes, see list below for number of quotes needed for the dollar amount of equipment or product you are purchasing:
    Quote Levels For Non-Contract Vendors:
    Requisition Total below $5,000 – 1 written quote (Email Acceptable)
    Requisition Total between $5,000 & $25,000 – documented quotes from 2 or more vendors by telephone, fax, or email.
    Requisition Total between $25,000 & $74,999 - 2 or more WRITTEN quotes .
    $75,000 & above – Formal sealed bid process such as Invitation to Bid, Request for Proposal, or Invitation to Negotiate (or approved exemption) - see our finance office for instructions
    For Contract Vendors:
    If the item is on State or University Contract,you only need to obtain the hard copy quote from the vendor you are ordering the item from. No additional quotes are necessary. Please include the quote with the “Order Request Form” so that it can be filed with the Purchase Order.
  4. What is a Sole Source? A Sole Source is an item or service is that is only available from ONE SOURCE, regardless of the price. This may be due to uniqueness, newness on the market, the item being patent protected, etc. A lower price, faster delivery time and/or preferring to do business with a company is not a valid reason for selecting a particular company. Purchasing can only accept a true Sole Source as justification to waive the bid requirements for purchases over $50,000.
  5. How do I prepare a Sole Source? The first step is for the Principle Investigator (PI) and/or Program Administrator to determine what is needed to accomplish the research/project. (We in Purchasing rely on the PI's to be the "resident authority" on the item they are wanting). Once the item has been identified as only available from one source, then the vendor should provide a written quotation. Please note that written quotes are normally only good for 30 days and Purchasing will return the requisition and attachments if the time period has expired. Once the quote is received, the Sole Source Certification Form located at http://www.purchasing.ufl.edu/Sole_source_.doc should be typed and all items should be answered thoroughly, in layman's terms. Do not leave any question blank. The PI should then sign the Sole Source Certification under the "Department Approval, Principle Investigator's Signature" heading. The "Purchasing Approval, Purchasing Agent's Signature and Authorized Signature"lines will be signed by Purchasing.

GATOR 1 ID CARD:
If a new employee or visitor needs a Gator 1 ID Card please see Jenny Manning or Donna Roberts. One of them will prepare a form for the employee or visitor to take to the Gator 1 Central Bookstore & Welcome Center, which is located in the Reitz Union on the Ground Floor.
We will need the following information when completing the paperwork :
  • Who will be paying for the ID card? If the Department or supervisor is paying for this, then we will need a project identified. Please remember that most grants will not allow such overhead related costs.
  • This person will need to have a UF ID established. We will be able to complete a search in the UF Directory to obtain this if one already exists. If this has not been established, then we will require additional information from this person in order to create one (i.e., full legal name, date of birth, permanent home address, work location, work phone #, email address).
  • We will also need to know the person’s affiliation to the Department in order to best select an appropriate role at UF. This can affect whether he / she will have access to certain services at UF.
  • We will need to know the anticipated ending date of their association with the Department if not an employee.

MEMBERSHIPS

Please be aware that when paying for a membership the following must be completed before payment can be made:

  1. A current Open Record Certification for Payment of Membership form must be on file. This certifies that, in keeping with the State of Florida's public records requirements, the organization's records are open for inspection.
    A current list of memberships, Open Records Certification for Memberships can be found at http://fa.ufl.edu/uds/.
  2. A Request for Organizational Membership Approval form must be on file for each membership.
  3. Be sure to check the application for lobby fees and political action (PAC) fees as these are not allowed.

When using the P-card to pay for a membership or membership renewal, it is the cardholder's responsibility to ensure the above requirements are met before making the charge.

AUTHORITY TO SIGN CONTRACTS
As a reminder, departments should not sign contracts unless they have received delegated authority from President Machen to do so. If you sign a contract when you have no authority, you may become personally liable for the contract provisions.
 
Items That Require Environmental Health & Safety Approval Prior to Purchase

(Effective March 29, 2001)
On and off campus sites will be required to obtain pre-approval from Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) before purchasing the following:
  • Agricultural Farm Equipment >$1000 (Effective 3/5/08)
  • Agricultural Tractors >$1000 (Effective 3/5/08)
  • Air Purifying Respirators
  • All Terrain Vehicles (Effective 3/5/08)
  • Bench Top Fume Collection Cabinets
  • Biological Safety Cabinets
  • Boilers and Pressure Vessels
  • Bulk Storage Tanks
  • Cleaning of Kitchen Exhaust Systems
  • Compressed Gas Cabinets
  • Electron Microscopes (Effective 3/5/08)
  • Explosives and Fireworks
  • Eyewash Stations and Safety Showers
  • Fall Protection Devices (Harnesses and Lanyards)
  • Fire Suppression Systems
  • Fume Hoods
  • Gas Chromatographs
  • Kitchen Cooking Appliances
  • Kitchen Exhaust Systems
  • Lab Vectors (Effective 3/5/08)
  • Lasers
  • Liquid Scintillation Counters
  • Microwave Ovens for Labs
  • Ozone Generators
  • Personal Monitoring or Detection Equipment (Confined Space Meters, Gas Meters, Survey Meters, Dosimeters, etc.)
  • Portable Air Cleaning Devices
  • Radioactive Materials
  • Refrigerators and Freezers
  • Repairs of equipment that require it to be sent off campus (certification of cleaning and decontaminating will be required.)
  • Self Contained Breathing Apparatus
  • X-Ray Machines
In addition, the following specific chemicals will need EH&S approval: Dioxins Tri-, Tetra- and Pentachlorophenols, or any items prohibited by law, regulation or policy.
